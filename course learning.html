Course Learning Reflections

1. Problems in Nature: Iteration, Recursion, and Backtracking

Iteration: Iterative processes like life cycles mimic loops in computing, such as traversing lists or cycles in algorithms.

Recursion: Self-similar natural patterns reflect recursive solutions in computing, like solving factorials or decision trees.

Backtracking: Systematic exploration mirrors pathfinding in mazes or solving N-Queens problems.

2. Space and Time Efficiency

Space Efficiency: Optimizing memory is key for embedded systems. Examples: Fenwick trees for range queries.

Time Efficiency: Speed is vital for real-time applications like GPS or search engines.

3. Algorithm Design Principles

Divide and Conquer: Merge Sort, Quick Sort. Applications: Dataset sorting, geometry.

Greedy Algorithms: Locally optimal choices for MSTs or network routing.

4. Graph Algorithms

Spanning Trees: Prim’s and Kruskal’s for network design.

Shortest Paths: Dijkstra’s and Bellman-Ford for navigation systems.
